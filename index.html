"They Need Us, We Need Them"

Four endangered animals—tigers, orangutans, eagles, and rhinos—face serious threats such as deforestation, poaching, and climate change. Their survival is increasingly threatened, but we can be their voice. Find information and ways to contribute to efforts to protect endangered animals

Sumatran Tiger

The Sumatran tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae) is a subspecies of tiger found only on the island of Sumatra, Indonesia. It is the smallest tiger compared to other subspecies, with a characteristic darker coat and tighter stripes. Sumatran tigers are critically endangered due to deforestation and poaching. Currently, their population is estimated to only be around 400-600 left in the wild.

Orang Utan

Orangutans are large primates found only in the rainforests of Sumatra and Kalimantan. They are known as one of the most intelligent great ape species, with the ability to use tools and have complex social relationships. Orangutans spend most of their lives in trees and have long arms to help them move from branch to branch. Unfortunately, their habitat continues to decrease due to illegal logging and oil palm plantations.

One horned rhinoceros

The One horned rhinoceros (Rhinoceros sondaicus) is one of the rarest mammals in the world. Currently, they are only found in Ujung Kulon National Park, Indonesia. This rhino has thick gray skin with an armor-like pattern and only one small horn which is smaller than other rhino species. The population is in critical condition, with less than 100 left in the wild.

Javanese Eagle

The Javanese eagle (Nisaetus bartelsi) is a bird of prey that is considered a national symbol of Indonesia. This bird is medium in size with wide wings, a long tail and a distinctive crest on its head. They live in the mountain forests of Java Island and are a symbol of Garuda in Indonesian culture. However, their existence is increasingly threatened due to deforestation and illegal hunting.

Endangnered Haven

Endangnered Haven literally means "Sanctuary for the Endangered." This term usually refers to a place or initiative dedicated to protecting endangered animal or plant species. The concept is to create a "haven" or safe place where threatened living creatures can live, reproduce, and be protected from external threats such as poaching, habitat loss, and climate change.
